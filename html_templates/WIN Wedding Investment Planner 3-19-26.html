This budgeting tool is designed to give you a clear starting point, and help you prioritize your wedding investment. The percentages are based on survey data and "average" weddings in New England and New York, but every wedding is different — and your priorities will ultimately shape how your budget comes together.

Use this as a guide to understand where couples typically spend, starting with the core costs most weddings require, and then layering in enhancements that can make your day feel even more personal and memorable.

💡 Pro tip: We know the budget doesn't totally add up — and that's okay!


Every wedding is a little different. Some require a planner, some include coordination. Some venues come with rentals, others have you bring everything in. So you might not need every line item here, and that's actually a good thing.


If you don't need something (like rentals), great — that frees up money to use somewhere else. Maybe that's a live artist, maybe it's a photo booth, maybe it's just a little breathing room in the budget.


There are also plenty of extras we didn't include here, like a guest lounge setup — or that welcome party tiki bar you saw on Pinterest (IYKYK). They're beautiful, but can add additional cost.

Before you start, ask yourselves:

Do you want guests talking about the food for years to come?
Is a packed dance floor your version of an epic night?
Do you value gorgeous photos you can pass down to your grandchildren?

So before you go too far, take a minute. Sit down with your fiancé, grab a piece of paper, and figure out what actually matters most to both of you. Write your priorities down in order — and remember them as you start to research vendors. Just set your budget, stick to your priorities, and be okay letting some things go so you can spend where it matters most.
